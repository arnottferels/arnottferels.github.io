Arnott Ferels is a computational designer and researcher, working at the intersection of computation, the built environment, and renewable energy. He actively seeks new opportunities to foster creativity in architecture and computation, focusing on improving efficiency and optimization to enhance the sustainability of the built environment.

His current research explores computational methods, including optimization algorithms, generative design, simulation-based design, urban simulation, BIM, and transit-oriented development (TOD). He is also investigating how data-driven strategies, machine learning, and AI can improve design methods by integrating them into a single, efficient, and cohesive workflow.
